Our Approach - Guided RSOAR

– Step 1: Remove process steps which are not needed &activities, that are non-value adding or not required or could be done differently. With our End 2 End process assessment approach we can help you uncover process steps which could be potentially eliminated or could be done differently.

– Step 2: Standardize Even though it might sound quite straight forward however as you would know it's not so simple. With the organizational complexity we have non-standard processes across regions, clusters, functions etc. The key ingredient to any successful automation project is to have a standard process. So let our teams also guide you with the standardization efforts where needed.

– Step 3: Optimize tie any loose ends in the current processes or your existing ERP landscape, We can recommend suggestions to do away with some of the least used features in your existing platforms , which may bring significant benefits.

– Step 4: Automateprocess steps that are typically error-prone or time-consuming with specialized – often small-scale and inexpensive – tools, either off-the-shelf or even custom developed. The term automation can certainly be used in a very broad context and often includes RPA and robotics, but doesn’t include GUI-based RPA in this context. It isn’t easy to keep up with the increasing market of these often niche problem-solving solutions, but in terms of efficiency gains, it’s definitely worth identifying and implementing them.

– Step 5: Robotize the remainder of any repetitive, rules-based, and often high-volume tasks, using either attended (front end) or unattended (back end) RPA, if a clear business case is identified for development and operation. Although other criteria should be taken into consideration, having a proven methodology for qualification and effort estimation – as well as other change management, governance, operation, and support measures – will help ensure positive implementation results.
